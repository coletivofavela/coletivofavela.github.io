Coletivo Favela is a land rights tool and guide that works to translate and spatialize urban policy in order for favela residents to be able to claim for their right to the city.

In the wake of the 2016 FIFA World Cup and the 2016 Summer Olympics, the municipality of Rio de Janeiro has neglected Federal Urban Policy in order to evict and displace thousands of favela residents from their homes. Now more than ever, communities need to unify in order to reclaim their rights in order to remain in their communities and to begin to reclaim the right to the city by improving their lives and their neighborhoods.

Coletivo Favela believes that collectivization in favelas is necessary for the health and prosperity of the community.

Favelas should be recognized by the municipality, the markets, and all inner-city residents as productive spaces and individuals that can provide economic, social and cultural capital to the city of Rio de Janeiro.

Favela residents should have access to federal urban policy and should be able to claim for their right to the city from the municipality of Rio de Janeiro.
